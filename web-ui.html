Open question
The browse panel (genre list + "Now Playing" mini-player) simply disappears below 900px with no replacement — meaning tablet and mobile users lose that functionality entirely rather than getting a mobile-adapted version. Worth a dedicated design pass: likely candidates are a "Browse" tab in the mobile bottom bar, or a collapsible drawer.

Accessibility & contrast standard

No accessibility standard exists anywhere in the current brand materials. Adopting the same WCAG 2.1 AA discipline used by mature systems (UNC's guide enforces this explicitly site-wide) — and checking it against real combinations already in the live product.

Standard
Minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large/bold text (18px+ or 14px+ bold). For the logo mark specifically, a lower bar of 2.25:1 is acceptable, following Netflix's own published standard for logo-over-image placements — since a logo needs to register, not be read letter by letter.
Cream on bg17.8:1 — pass
Lavender-light on bg6.1:1 — pass
Magenta2 on plum1.4:1 — fail

That third combination — a mid-purple on a darker purple — is a realistic mistake to make in a purple-only system precisely because everything is in-family and "looks" related. This is the practical risk of going all-purple: contrast has to be checked deliberately rather than assumed from hue alone.
